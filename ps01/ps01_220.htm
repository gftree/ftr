Notes for Robert Crow
Robert Crow

RogerBean20 added this on 28 Sep 2008 to ANCESTRY.CO.UK

The marriage of a Robert Crow and a Harriett White at Worsborough St. Mary's on 13/10/1835 and the death of a Robert Crow in 1846 (registered at Ecclesfield 22 125 Q2/1846) who was buried at Worsborough St. Mary's on 3/5/1846 has caused some confusion. This Robert Crow was born in Kexborough in 1812 (son of Timothy) and baptised at Darton on 14/4/1812. He did not marry Hannah White.

The Robert Crow who did do (date and location unknown but C1835) journeyed to Van Diemen's Land in 1842 and his last known whereabouts are on board a ship which took him from Hobart to Melbourne in 1852. The records for the former journey give his place of birth as County Tipperary and his age as being 25. Hannah White's second marriage could be seen as being bigamous being only 4 years after Robert went to Van Diemen's Land. She should have waited until 1849 as 7 years after the transportation of a convicted criminal was the relevant timescale for a legal remarriage.
Notes for Hannah (Spouse 1)
Hannah (White) Crow was separated from her husband Robert Crow as in 1842 he was transported to Van Diemens Land (modern day Tasmania) in Australia (on a convicts ship). She remained in Barnsley and remarried twice.
