Birth1815, Templemore, Co. Tipperary, Ireland [24]
Birth MemoAs a baby he most likely lived in the soldiers married quarters of the Richmond Barracks at Templemore in Co. Tipperary, Ireland as his father was a Private in the 25th Regiment. “Templemore is well known as the location of the Garda College, which has been the national training faculty for the Garda Síochána na hÉireann since 1964. The complex has a long and interesting history as a military barracks, having opened in 1813 as Richmond Barracks.” — http://tipperarystudies.ie/2016/10/13/podcast-germ...15-by-john-reynolds/ “Its construction was started and completed in the first decade of 1800. Sir John Carden also gave a training and recreation ground of 40 acres adjoining. The barracks had two squares, surrounded by company lines, stores, married quarters, officers mess block, church, military prison and hospital. The site was surrounded by a high wall, with projecting fire positions at each corner. It had accommodation as follows: C/O’s house and garden, quarters for two field officers, 23 other officers, married quarters for 48 other ranks, and for 767 other unmarried personnel. It also had 36 hospital beds, 15 guard room cells, and stabling for 27 officers’ horses.” — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McCan_Barracks
Baptism10 Oct 1815, Templemore, Co. Tipperary, Ireland [24] Age: <1
Bapt MemoHis name was mistranscribed as “Robert Croe” on his 1815 Baptism Parish Record. Notably his parents names are the same as those on his 1887 death certificate in Australia.
Census6 Jun 1841, Barnsley, Yorkshire [25], [5] Age: 26
Census MemoHe was working as a Linen Weaver and living in Wilson Street, Barnsley (Barnsley, District 6 - The street is probably in Wombwell and exists today - or possibly Hoyland). His age is stated incorrectly as 20 when he was 25. Their third child Mary b: April 1841 is not mentioned on the 6 June 1841 census so she likely died as an infant.
Court2 Mar 1842, York Assizes (Court) [26] Age: 27
Court MemoHe was convicted and sentenced to 15 years (with transportation to Van Diemens Land now known as Tasmania, Australia) for “Robbery from the Person with Violence”. The crime was committed with James Betts who was also convicted and transported. He had been imprisoned three times before (probably in Barnsley or Ireland) and was of “very bad character in every respect” according to his Convict Record. He had neglected his family and 1 month of his sentence was for this. 2 months of his sentence was for breaking into a warehouse. He was fined 6 shillings for assault in “knocking down Mr Dyson”. He was described at age 26 as: 5 foot 4 inch in height, Dark Brown Hair, Grey Eyes, Fresh Complexion, Long Head, Broad Forehead and Chin, Medium Nose, Black Eyebrows.
Susan-2 Ship24 Apr 1842, Plymouth, England [27], [26] Age: 27
Susan-2 Ship MemoHe was transported from England to Australia. Official documents record: “Departure on Vessel Susan (2) / Arrived 25 07 1842 VDL / Sailed 24 04 1842 Plymouth / Days 92 / Embarked Males 299 / Arrived Males 297 / Master Hy Neatby / Surgeon Goe T Moxey” (from the book: The Convict Ships, 1787-1868, by Charles Bateson. 2nd ed. 1974).
Immigration25 Jul 1842, Hobart, Tasmania Age: 27
Immi MemoHe arrives in Hobart on the ship Susan-2 from Plymouth, England after a 3 month voyage. Starts his life as a convict in the famous penal colony of Van Diemens Land.
Pardon24 Aug 1850, Launceston, Tasmania [28] Age: 35
Pardon Memo8 years into his 15 year sentence a Conditional Pardon (CP) is recommended on 20 Aug 1850 (written in red on his Convict Record) and granted. This is mentioned in two Launceston, Tasmania newspapers on the same day.
Gold Rush?1852, Near Ballarat, Victoria, Australia Age: 37
Gold Rush? MemoIt is quite possible (but not proved) that Robert Crow participated in the Australian Gold Rush which peaked at the same time as he arrived at its epicentre of Melbourne. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victorian_gold_rush Records exist of a Robert Crow (not proved as ours) having children with a local Avoca, Vic. woman in the 1860s. Pursuing the “gold rush” in his circumstances and location would have been a logical choice. “Like Ballarat and many other Victorian towns, Avoca sprang into being suddenly in the 1850s with the discovery of gold. Gold was first found in Victoria in 1849 in the Pyrenees Ranges near Avoca. But it was not for another two years that the first discovery of any importance took place. In 1851 a shepherd called James Esmond found gold at Clunes, forty kilometres from present-day Avoca, setting off a gold rush to the region.” — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avoca,_Victoria#Early_settlement_and_the_gold_rush

Hulk Ship13 Mar 1852, Van Diemens Land (Tasmania), Australia [29] Age: 37
Hulk Ship Memo“Sentence Expired”. Before departing to Melbourne later as a free man, Robert Crow is now on the Joseph Somes Hulk Ship in Van Diemens Land (Tasmania), which probably served as a half-way house (a proof-point that was quoted on his later sailing on the ship called Swift to Melbourne).
Swift Ship31 Mar 1852, Launceston, Australia Age: 37
Swift Ship MemoNow a free man, he travelled from VDL to Melbourne on “Swift” ship. Records state: “Crow R in Steerage on Swift departing 31 Mar 1852 from Launceston to Melbourne [from] J Soames [sp] / Free by servitude.” — Source: POL220/1/1 p637.
Free ManApr 1852, Melbourne, Victoria Age: 37
Free Man MemoHe started a new life as a free man in or around the gold rush boomtown of Melbourne, Victoria.
DeathJun 1887, Melbourne Benevolent Asylum, Victoria, Australia (Hotham West) [30], [31] Age: 72
Death MemoHe died in North West Melbourne. The hospital still exists in Victoria but not at this location. The cemetery where he was likely buried no longer exists as the site has been built over. “North Melbourne's first institutions were built in the 1840s … In the 1850s a Benevolent Asylum was built between Abbotsford and Curzon Streets, coinciding with the desire to find space to accommodate the growing population from the gold rush. (The Asylum remained a feature of the area until its move to Cheltenham in 1911.) In 1859 the area was named Hotham borough, after the governor of Victoria Charles Hotham. … Hotham had a population of over 7,000. … On 26 August 1887 the Borough was renamed North Melbourne Town, after the completion of the imposing North Melbourne Town Hall and the Metropolitan Meat Market.” — https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Melbourne,_Victoria

Separation1842, Barnsley, Yorkshire
Sep MemoHannah (White) Crow was separated from her husband Robert Crow when in 1842 he was transported to Van Diemens Land. She remained in Barnsley and remarried twice. “Hannah White's second marriage could be seen as being bigamous being only 4 years after Robert [CROW] went to Van Diemen's Land. She should have waited until 1849 as 7 years after the transportation of a convicted criminal was the relevant timescale for a legal remarriage.” (RogerBean20, 28 Sep 2008, ancestry.co.uk).
